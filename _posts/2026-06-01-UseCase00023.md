**User Story:** As a researcher, I want to document my analysis of a recorded interview so that I can better understand the dialectic process that is occurring.

**Description:** When listening to a recorded conversation, whether it is an interview, a field recording, or other organically recorded conversation, the dialectic process of the conversation can be analyzed to study what is being said, how it is being said, and why it is being said. Annotations allow this analysis to be clearly and interactively matched up to timestamps. Relevant fields include English, Humanities, Social Sciences, and Journalism.

**Ingredients**
- A recorded conversation (interview, field recording, etc.)
- Annotations (analysis of the conversation)
- Transcript (optional)
- Tags (optional)

**Method**
1. Listen to the recorded conversation and analyze what is being said, how it is being said, why it is being said, etc. - document **timestamps** of all observations and analyses
2. Upload audiovisual works to AVAnnotate and add analysis as **annotations**, with associated **timestamps**, following [Quick Start: How to use AVAnnotate](https://av-annotate.org/how-to-use/) guide
3. Add **transcript** of conversation as **annotations**, if desired
4. Create and add **tags** to annotations, if desired, following Quick Start guide
5. Create one or more pages documenting and expanding upon analysis, if desired

**Example:** [Listening to Betty Hill](https://leobriggs.github.io/listeningtobetty/) by Leo Briggs
