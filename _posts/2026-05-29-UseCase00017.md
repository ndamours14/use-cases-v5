**User Story:** As a documentary interviewer, I want to present an annotated version of an interview I have conducted so that I can provide additional context and background information.

**Description:** While interviews can be valuable and interesting audiovisual items in their own right, they often benefit from additional context and background about the interviewee and/or the topic of the interview. This information can be included as annotations connected to specific moments in the interview. Relevant fields include Documentary Production, Social Studies, Arts, Sciences, and Humanities.

**Ingredients**
- An audio or audiovisual interview recording
- Annotations
- Transcript (optional)
- Tags (optional)

**Method**
1. Listen to the interview, taking notes to identify speakers, topics, etc., with associated **timestamps**
2. Gather context and background information on interviewee and/or interview topic, with associated **timestamps** for each piece of information
3. Upload audio or audiovisual file to AVAnnotate and add information as **annotations**, following [Quick Start: How to use AVAnnotate](https://av-annotate.org/how-to-use/) guide
4. If desired, generate a transcript of the interview or gather an existing transcript if it exists - include transcript as **annotations** as well
5. Create and add **tags** to annotations, if desired, following Quick Start guide

**Example:** [Lester Hamlet in Depth](https://avannotate.github.io/LesterHamlet/events/interview-with-jack-riordan-entrevista-con/) by Jack Devry Riordan
