**User Story:** As a scholar working with audio recordings of literary works, I want to create a critical “audio edition” of a work so that I can present an audio recording with the same institutional value as a print edition of a work.

**Description:** As explored in the article [“Modeling the Audio Edition with Mavis Gallant’s 1984 Reading of “Grippes and Poche””](https://www.erudit.org/fr/revues/esc/2020-v46-n2-3-4-esc09328/1111320ar.pdf) by Kate Moffatt, Kandice Sharren, and Michelle Levy, critical editions of literary works carry a certain institutional value–and critical editions need not be text-based. An audio edition of a literary work, such as an author’s reading of their work accompanied by additional context, biographical information, and/or critical analysis, can form a critical audio edition of a work. AVAnnotate can be used to present and annotate this critical audio edition. Relevant fields include English, Humanities, Literature, Sound Studies, and Poetry.

**Ingredients**
- One or more audio recordings of a literary work or works
- Annotations for each recording (contextual information, biographical information, and/or critical analysis)
- Introduction to audio edition
- Bibliography for audio edition
- Tags (optional)

**Method**
1. Gather contextual information, biographical information, and/or a critical analysis about the work, its creator, and the recording
  - Context and analysis can focus on the literary work as well as on the circumstances of the recording
2. Use this information to create annotations for each resource, with **timestamps** for each annotation
3. Upload recording(s) to AVAnnotate and add **annotations**, following [Quick Start: How to use AVAnnotate](https://av-annotate.org/how-to-use/) guide
4. Create and add **tags** to annotations, if desired, following Quick Start guide
5. Create a home page, to introduce the critical audio edition, as well as to provide a bibliography for the audio edition
