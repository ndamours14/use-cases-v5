**User Story:** As a researcher studying literary history, I want to capture the live reactions of viewers watching literary recordings so that I can enrich these videos with additional context.

**Description:** Much like the director’s commentary that accompanies some films, reactions and commentary can be added to videos as annotations to provide additional background and context. A video can be played for a viewer, particularly a viewer who was involved in the production of the video or has interesting insights into the video. Their reactions can be recorded, along with relevant timestamps for their reaction. These reactions can then be added to the video as written annotations. Relevant fields include History, Film History, English, and Social Sciences.

**Ingredients**
- 1 or more videos
- 1 or more viewers with relevant insight into the video(s)
- Annotations based on live reactions of viewer(s)
- Tags (optional)

**Method**
1. Play the video(s) for your viewer(s), encouraging them to react either verbally or in written form; take notes on their reactions, including relevant **timestamps** for each reaction
2. Create written **annotations** based on viewer(s) reactions, commentary, etc.
3. Upload the video(s) to AVAnnotate and add reactions as **annotations**, following [Quick Start: How to use AVAnnotate](https://av-annotate.org/how-to-use/) guide
4. Create and add **tags** to annotations, if desired, following Quick Start guide
