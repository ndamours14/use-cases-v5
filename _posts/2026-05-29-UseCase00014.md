**User Story:** As a graduate student studying Black queer history through music, I want to present my thesis in a multimedia format so that I can showcase relevant music videos alongside the text of my thesis.

**Description:** Many thesis projects may involve multimedia elements, including films, research videos, audio recordings, music, etc. AVAnnotate provides a platform that allows students to present text alongside video and audio, and allows for that video and audio to be annotated to clearly demonstrate arguments, observations, etc. Any thesis that includes or makes reference to audiovisual elements can be presented via AVAnnotate. Relevant fields include Film Studies, Sound Studies, Humanities, and Sciences.

**Ingredients**
- Thesis text
- 1 or more audiovisual files related to thesis
- Annotations
- Tags (optional)

**Method**
1. Gather thesis text, divided into sections which can be distinct **pages** of an AVAnnotate project; this can be an entire thesis or only one portion of a thesis
2. Gather relevant information, arguments, observations, etc. for each audiovisual file, with associated **timestamps** for each piece of information
3. Upload audiovisual files to AVAnnotate and add informations as **annotations**, following [Quick Start: How to use AVAnnotate](https://av-annotate.org/how-to-use/) guide
4. Create and add **tags** to annotations, if desired, following Quick Start guide
5. Create as many **pages** as desired to contain text of thesis, following Quick Start guide; text can accompany audiovisual files or be hosted on separate pages

**Example:** [Black LGBTQ+ Soundscape Ecologies and the Politics of Illegitimated Language: Ballroom, Mixes and Beyond](https://alyssaf-j.github.io/masters-report/) by Alyssa Frick-Jenkins (this project was shared on the AVAnnotate GitHub discussion board - will seek permission from Alyssa to share more widely if included on a public use case website)
