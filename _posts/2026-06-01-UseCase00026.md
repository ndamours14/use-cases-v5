**User Story:** As a student studying performative speech, I want to annotate audiovisual recordings of performances so that I can document the effects of performance on meaning and understanding.

**Description:** Performance has a deep impact on the meaning of words. The way that a speech, comedy act, poem, etc. is performed influences its meaning and how it is understood. AVAnnotate can be used to document observations about a performance, by pinpointing specific moments in a recording and adding observations in the form of annotations. Relevant fields include English, Social Sciences, Rhetoric, and Performing Arts.

**Ingredients**
- Audiovisual recording(s) of a performance (from stand-up comedy, film, radio, television, political speech, etc.)
- Annotations (observations about performance)
- Tags (optional)

**Method**
1. Watch performance recording(s) and make note of all observations related to the performance, as well as associated **timestamps** of all observations
  - The steps outlined in [these lesson plans](https://jentery.github.io/SWDemo/output_documents/ASSIGNMENTS_FOR_FRS4_.html) can be used to deeply engage with observing and analyzing performances
2. Upload performance recording(s) to AVAnnotate and add observations as **annotations**, with associated **timestamps**, following [Quick Start: How to use AVAnnotate](https://av-annotate.org/how-to-use/) guide
3. Create and add **tags** to annotations, if desired, following Quick Start guide
