**User Story:** As a research collective, we want to present several multimedia projects collectively so that we can share our group’s works as one centralized project.

**Description:** Collectives who are producing a variety of multimedia projects, such as films, videos, podcasts, or research videos, may wish to create one centralized project that showcases multiple creators’ works in one place. An AVAnnotate project can act as a hub for many creators’ projects. Relevant fields include Film Studies, Sound Studies, Humanities, and Sciences.

**Ingredients**
- Multiple audiovisual files from different creators
- Text introducing both the collective and individual projects
- Annotations
- Tags (optional)

**Method**
1. Gather both introductory text which serves to introduce the collective and their projects, as well as separate text introducing and expanding on individual projects, which can be split among multiple **pages**
2. Gather supplementary information, context, etc. for each audiovisual file, with associated **timestamps** for each piece of information
3. Upload audiovisual files to AVAnnotate and add information as **annotations**, following [Quick Start: How to use AVAnnotate](https://av-annotate.org/how-to-use/) guide
4. Create and add **tags** to annotations, if desired, following Quick Start guide
5. Create a main introductory **page**, following Quick Start guide, as well as linked pages for each creators’ project(s)
  - Pages may be [auto-generated](https://avannotate.github.io/documentation/pages/auto/) or [customized](https://avannotate.github.io/documentation/pages/custom/) - more information about building an AVAnnotate site can be found in the [AVAnnotate Documentation](https://avannotate.github.io/documentation/pages/projects/)

**Example:** [The SpokenWeb Digital Anthology](https://hipstas.github.io/spokenweb-anthology/) edited by Zoe Bursztajn-Illingworth and Tanya Clement
