**User Story:** As a Women's and Gender Studies student, I want to take notes on video essays about toxic masculinities in popular culture so that I can draw on these video essays as sources of information for my studies.

**Description:** Video essays are an emerging form of critical analysis, particularly in the context of analysing popular culture. Students may wish to draw on information from video essays in their studies. Tools like AVAnnotate can assist with note-taking for video essays, allowing for a video to be “marked up” and annotated, much like a student or scholar may annotate a written essay they are studying. Relevant fields include English, Social Studies, and Humanities.

**Ingredients**
- One or more video essays
- Notes about video essays
- Tags (optional)

**Method**
1. Critically watch video essays and pause to take notes on the contents of the video essays while watching, making note of **timestamps** for all notes
2. Upload video essay(s) to AVAnnotate and add notes as **annotations**, following [Quick Start: How to use AVAnnotate](https://av-annotate.org/how-to-use/) guide
  - Alternatively, the video essay can be uploaded to AVAnnotate *before* taking notes, allowing you to pause and add notes directly on AVAnnotate as you go
3. Create and add **tags** to annotations, if desired, following Quick Start guide

**Example:** [Complicit Masculinities](https://em-jense.github.io/dhcreation/events/complicit-masculinities/) by Emily Jensen
