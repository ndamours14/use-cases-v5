**User Story:** As an exhibition curator, I want to create a digital exhibition that includes annotations so that I can provide online access to my collection with interesting and comprehensive information.

**Description:** Exhibitions curators for museums, libraries, special collections, archives, and galleries often create digital exhibitions to showcase collections for a broad online audience. AVAnnotate allows digital items to be presented on a website in an interesting, interactive format. Any audiovisual items can be annotated to provide additional information. Relevant fields include Art, History, Humanities, and Sciences.

**Ingredients**
- A collection of born-digital or digitized items from a library, archive, museum, etc.
- Information about the collection and each individual item
- Annotations for any audiovisual items in the collection
- Tags (optional)

**Method**
1. Plan digital exhibition - this can involve selecting items to display, digitizing any items that have not yet been digitized, creating a narrative for the exhibition, collecting information on items in the exhibition, etc.
2. Gather information about each audiovisual item in the exhibition, with associated timestamps for each piece of information
3. Upload audiovisual items to AVAnnotate and add information as annotations, following Quick Start: How to use AVAnnotate guide
4. Create and add tags to annotations, if desired, following Quick Start guide
5. Create a home page and any additional pages for the digital exhibition, introducing and showcasing the collection
6. Any non-adiovisual items, such as static images and PDFs, can be included on the main pages of the exhibition or hosted on their own individual pages
