**User Story:** As an author of a paper about a history podcast, I want to include annotated podcast episodes with the paper so that I can provide more insight into the topic of my paper.

**Description:** When a paper addresses or deals with any kind of audio or audiovisual content, giving the reader direct access to the audio or audiovisual content allows them to better understand the contents of the paper. Including annotations with the audio or audiovisual content allows for an even greater connection between the paper and the content it addresses. Relevant fields include History, Archival Studies, Arts, English, Sciences, and Humanities.

**Ingredients**
- A paper which addresses any kind of audio or audiovisual recording(s)
- One or more audio or audiovisual recordings, addressed in the paper
- Annotations
- Tags (optional)

**Method**
1. Gather paper and all relevant audio or audiovisual recordings addressed in the paper
2. Gather information about the recordings, either drawn directly from the paper or supplementary information, with associated **timestamps** for each piece of information
3. Upload recordings to AVAnnotate and add information as **annotations**, following [Quick Start: How to use AVAnnotate](https://av-annotate.org/how-to-use/) guide
4. Create and add **tags** to annotations, if desired, following Quick Start guide
5. Create a page or multiple pages for the paper - these pages can be [customized](https://avannotate.github.io/documentation/pages/custom/), and may contain either plain text or embedded images, audiovisual elements, etc.
6. Create pages for each annotate recording - these pages can be [auto-generated](https://avannotate.github.io/documentation/pages/auto/) or [customized](https://avannotate.github.io/documentation/pages/custom/)
  - Hyperlinks in the paper can be used to link the reader directly to annotated recordings
  - More information about building an AVAnnotate site can be found in the [AVAnnotate Documentation](https://avannotate.github.io/documentation/pages/projects/)

**Example:** [Annotating The Last Archive](https://avannotate.github.io/thelastarchive/) by Ali Gunnells and Sam Turner (this project was shared on the AVAnnotate GitHub discussion board by Tanya Clement - will seek permission from Tanya, Ali, and/or Sam to share more widely if included on a public use case website)
