**User Story:** As a wildlife researcher, I want to highlight incidental sounds in my field recordings so that I can better understand the environment being recorded.

**Description:** During a field recording, many incidental background sounds may be picked up in the recording. These sounds can be highlighted, categorized, and defined using annotations in order to better understand the environment and context of the recording. Relevant fields include Natural Sciences, Wildlife Research, and Social Sciences.

**Ingredients**
- 1 or more field recordings
- Annotations
- Tags (optional)

**Method**
1. Identify incidental sounds and create annotations to define and/or categorize these sounds, with associated **timestamps** for each sound
2. Upload field recording(s) to AVAnnotate and add **annotations** for each identified sound, following [Quick Start: How to use AVAnnotate](https://av-annotate.org/how-to-use/) guide
3. Create and add **tags** to annotations, if desired, following Quick Start guide

**Example:** [Zora Neale Hurston’s WPA field recordings in Jacksonville, FL (1939)](https://tanyaclement.github.io/znh-1939/) by Tanya Clement
