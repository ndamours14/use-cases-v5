**User Story:** As a professor, I want to teach my students to use AVAnnotate so that they can use the platform to create multimedia class projects.

**Description:** Professors can use AVAnnotate as a teaching tool, to introduce students to the concept and utility of AV annotation. Teaching students to use AVAnnotate also allows them to use the platform to create multimedia class projects, for the class at hand and in future classes where they may be dealing with audio and/or video content. Relevant fields include English, Sound Studies, Social Studies, and Humanities.

**Ingredients**
- A class project utilising audio or audiovisual recordings
- Audio or audiovisual recordings (generated by students)
- Annotations (generated by students)
- Tags (generated by students)

**Method**
1. Generate a class project which involves the creation of audio or audiovisual recordings, the creation of annotations and tags for the recordings, and the construction of an AVAnnotate project
2. Guide students through the creation their audio or audiovisual recordings, as well as the gathering of supplementary information (**annotations**), with associated **timestamps** for each piece of information
3. Guide students through uploading their audio or audiovisual files to AVAnnotate and adding their information as **annotations**, following [Quick Start: How to use AVAnnotate](https://av-annotate.org/how-to-use/) guide
4. Guide students through creating and adding **tags** to annotations, if desired, following Quick Start guide
5. Create a landing page that links to all student AVAnnotate projects in one place, if desired
